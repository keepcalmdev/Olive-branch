Our History

The Olive Branch Insurance company was formed in 1873 by twelve farmers in Kramer, Nebraska. With a handshake, the insurance company began and provided coverage against fire and lightening losses. Later, the company gravitated towards the Sprague-Martell area.

In May 1952 the company adopted the wind and extended coverage endorsements which then became apart of its standard policy. The Articles of Incorporation were filed in 1969 and were signed by company affiliates Henry Sieck, J. Carl Sitler, Harvey Beck, Walter Heusinkvelt, John Kersten, Donald Egger, Kenneth Clough, Andrew Benes, and Phil Buel.

In February 1982 the Board decided to set up an office in the house located at 1600 West 3rd Street in Sprague, NE.

In September 1995 the Board adopted and had approved a new farm and town policy which provided basic and broad coverage with an option of replacement cost and mobile agriculture coverage.

In January 2003 Olive Branch moved into a new office at 18930 Market St in Sprague, NE. The new building was built energy efficient and provides more room to serve our policyholders.
